THE SEVEN SACRAMENTS

The sacraments are efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church, by which divine life is dispensed to us.

1. BAPTISM: The gateway to life in the Spirit. Baptism frees from original sin, makes us children of God, and incorporates us into the Church.

2. CONFIRMATION: Perfects baptismal grace. The confirmed are more perfectly bound to the Church and are enriched with a special strength of the Holy Spirit to witness to Christ.

3. EUCHARIST: The source and summit of the Christian life. In the Eucharist, Christ is truly, really, and substantially present under the appearances of bread and wine.

4. PENANCE (RECONCILIATION): Through this sacrament, the faithful obtain pardon from God's mercy for the offenses committed against him, and are reconciled with the Church.

5. ANOINTING OF THE SICK: Those who are seriously ill receive strength, peace, and courage to endure their suffering in union with Christ's Passion.

6. HOLY ORDERS: By this sacrament, men are ordained as bishops, priests, or deacons to serve the Church as ministers of Christ.

7. MATRIMONY: The covenant by which a man and a woman establish between themselves a partnership of the whole of life, ordered to the good of the spouses and the procreation and education of offspring.
