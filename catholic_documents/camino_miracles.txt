THE MIRACLE OF THE HANGED MAN (Santo Domingo de la Calzada)
A young pilgrim was falsely accused of theft and hanged. After several days, his parents heard his voice from the gallows, saying St. James was holding him up. The town magistrate found the pilgrim alive and went to retrieve a chicken and rooster from his dinner, which then stood up and crowed. A rooster and hen are kept in the cathedral to this day.

THE APPARITION OF ST. JAMES AT CLAVIJO (c. 844)
King Ramiro I was fighting against the Moors. St. James appeared on a white horse, leading the Christian army to victory. This legend solidified St. James as "Matamoros" (Moor-slayer) and patron of Spain.

OUR LADY OF THE FISH (Padrón)
The boat carrying St. James' body arrived at Padrón, where the disciples tied the boat to a stone. That stone grew into an altar, now in the church of Santiago de Padrón. Our Lady appeared here, holding a fish, symbolizing the new life brought by the faith.

THE MIRACULOUS CROSS OF THE ANGELS
During the dedication of the first church in Santiago, angels are said to have carved the altar cross. This cross is preserved in the Cathedral of Oviedo and is carried on the Camino Primitivo.

ST. JAMES AND THE ZEBEDEE BROTHERS
Tradition holds that St. James and St. John were called "Sons of Thunder" because they were the first to hear the "thunder" of the Gospel. Their zeal carried them to the ends of the earth.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "These signs will accompany those who believe." Miracles on the Camino remind us that God is present in the journey and that faith can move mountains.
