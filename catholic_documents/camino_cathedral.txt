SOURCE: The Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela

HISTORY
- First church built by King Alfonso II (9th century)
- Romanesque cathedral built from 1075-1128
- Gothic, Baroque, and Neoclassical additions over centuries
- Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site (1985)

THE FAÇADE
- The Baroque Obradoiro façade (18th century)
- Pilgrims enter through the Holy Door during Jubilee Years
- The statue of St. James welcomes pilgrims

THE RELICS
- St. James' remains are in the crypt under the main altar
- Discovered in 813, authenticated by popes
- Rediscovered in 1879, confirmed by Pope Leo XIII (1884)
- Also contains relics of disciples Theodore and Athanasius

THE BOTAFUMEIRO
- Giant incense burner used during solemn Masses
- Weighs 53 kg (117 lbs), swings on 65-meter ropes
- Eight men (tiraboleiros) operate it
- Symbol of purification and prayer rising to heaven

THE PORTICO OF GLORY (Pórtico de la Gloria)
- Romanesque masterpiece by Master Mateo (1188)
- Depicts Christ in glory surrounded by saints
- Pilgrims traditionally placed their hand on the Tree of Jesse
- Five-fingered indentations worn by centuries of pilgrims

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The Cathedral is the earthly goal of the pilgrimage, but the true goal is heaven. St. James points us toward the eternal Jerusalem.
