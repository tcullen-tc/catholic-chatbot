SOURCE: St. John the Evangelist - The Beloved Disciple

KEY FACTS
- Brother of St. James the Greater
- Son of Zebedee and Salome
- Called "the Beloved Disciple" (John 13:23; 19:26; 20:2)
- Author of the Gospel of John, three epistles, and Revelation
- The only apostle not martyred
- Died of natural causes in Ephesus (c. 100 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- Beloved Disciple: John is the one who reclined next to Jesus at the Last Supper (John 13:23)
- At the Cross: Jesus entrusts Mary to John: "Behold, your mother" (John 19:26-27)
- First to the Tomb: John outruns Peter to the empty tomb (John 20:3-8)
- Recognizes the Risen Lord: John recognizes Jesus on the shore of Tiberias (John 21:7)
- Writer of Revelation: John receives and records the visions of Revelation while exiled on Patmos (Revelation 1:9)

TEACHINGS
John's writings emphasize love: "God is love" (1 John 4:8). His Gospel is the most theological, focusing on the divinity of Christ. He teaches us to rest in Jesus' love.

MAJOR THEMES IN JOHN'S WRITINGS
- The Word made flesh (John 1:1-14)
- Jesus as the Bread of Life (John 6)
- Jesus as the Good Shepherd (John 10)
- The New Commandment: Love one another (John 13:34-35)

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Little children, love one another."
"God so loved the world that he gave his only Son." (John 3:16)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
John teaches us that love is the heart of the Gospel. To be loved by Jesus is to be called to love others.
