SOURCE: St. Catherine of Genoa (1447-1510)
Mystic

KEY TEACHINGS

PURGATORY AS LOVE
Catherine had a unique vision of purgatory. She saw it not as a place of punishment but as the burning away of all that is not love, so that the soul can be fully united with God.

GOD'S LOVE
She taught that God's love is so pure that any imperfection in the soul causes suffering—not because God punishes, but because love itself purifies.

UNION WITH GOD
Her masterpiece, "Treatise on Purgatory," describes the soul's longing for God. She experienced deep union with God even while serving the sick in hospitals.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"God is all love; and he who loves, hopes, and trusts, is in heaven."
"No tongue can tell, no heart can understand, the peace that God gives to the soul that trusts in him."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Be perfect, as your heavenly Father is perfect." St. Catherine shows us that God's love is so pure that it purifies us, making us ready for eternal union with him.

Source: Treatise on Purgatory; Life of St. Catherine of Genoa
