HISTORY OF THE ROSARY
The Rosary as we know it developed over centuries. Tradition holds that St. Dominic received the Rosary from Our Lady in the 13th century to combat the Albigensian heresy.

THE BATTLE OF LEPANTO (1571)
Pope St. Pius V asked all of Europe to pray the Rosary for victory against the Ottoman fleet. The Christian fleet won a decisive victory. Pope Pius V established the Feast of Our Lady of Victory, later renamed Our Lady of the Rosary (October 7).

PROMISES OF THE ROSARY
According to tradition, Our Lady gave 15 promises to those who faithfully pray the Rosary:
- Protection from calamity
- Not to be overcome by sin
- Purgatory will be relieved
- All who pray it will be saved
- They will obtain all they ask
- They will not perish by an unhappy death
- They will have light and grace

POPES ON THE ROSARY
Pope Leo XIII wrote 12 encyclicals on the Rosary, calling it the "perfect prayer."
Pope St. John Paul II added the Luminous Mysteries in 2002.
Pope Francis: "The Rosary is a prayer that always accompanies me."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The Rosary is the prayer of the Gospel. In praying it, we meditate on the life of Christ through the eyes of his mother.
