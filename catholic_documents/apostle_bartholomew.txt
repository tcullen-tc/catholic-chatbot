SOURCE: St. Bartholomew (Nathanael) - The Sincere Israelite

KEY FACTS
- Also known as Nathanael
- Called by Philip to meet Jesus
- Known for his sincerity
- Patron saint of Armenia, butchers, and neurological diseases
- Martyrdom: Flayed alive and crucified (c. 70 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- The Call: Philip tells Nathanael, "We have found the one Moses wrote about" (John 1:45)
- Nathanael's Sarcasm: "Can anything good come from Nazareth?" (John 1:46)
- Jesus Sees Nathanael: Jesus says, "Here truly is an Israelite in whom there is no deceit" (John 1:47)
- Nathanael's Confession: "Rabbi, you are the Son of God! You are the King of Israel!" (John 1:49)
- The Fig Tree: Jesus says, "I saw you while you were still under the fig tree before Philip called you." (John 1:48)

TEACHINGS
Bartholomew was honest and direct. He doubted at first but became a believer when Jesus showed he knew him. His sincerity made him open to grace.

TRADITION
Bartholomew is said to have preached in India and Armenia. His martyrdom by flaying is why he is often depicted holding his skin.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Can anything good come from Nazareth?" (John 1:46)
"Rabbi, you are the Son of God! You are the King of Israel!" (John 1:49)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus sees us as we are and loves us anyway. He calls us by name and invites us to follow.
