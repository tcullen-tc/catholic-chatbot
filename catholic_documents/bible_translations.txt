MAJOR BIBLE TRANSLATIONS

SEPTUAGINT (c. 250 BC)
The first translation of the Hebrew Bible into Greek. Made by Jewish scholars in Alexandria, Egypt. Used by early Christians and quoted frequently in the New Testament.

VULGATE (AD 382-405)
Translated by St. Jerome from Hebrew and Greek into Latin. Became the standard Bible of the Western Church for over 1000 years.

KING JAMES VERSION (1611)
Commissioned by King James I of England. Translated by 47 scholars working in six companies. Known for its literary beauty. Remains influential today.

DOUAY-RHEIMS BIBLE (1582-1610)
The first complete English Catholic Bible translation. New Testament published at Rheims in 1582; Old Testament at Douay in 1610. Based on the Latin Vulgate.

REVISED STANDARD VERSION - CATHOLIC EDITION (1966)
An adaptation of the RSV approved for Catholic use. Incorporates the deuterocanonical books and reflects Catholic tradition.

NEW AMERICAN BIBLE (1970)
The first Catholic English Bible translated from the original languages. Revised in 1986 and 2011.
