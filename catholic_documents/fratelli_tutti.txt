On Fraternity and Social Friendship

KEY TEACHINGS

"FRATELLI TUTTI" – "All brothers." Saint Francis of Assisi called his followers to a fraternal love that transcends geographical and distance barriers. This is the heart of the Gospel.

A DREAM WITHOUT BORDERS
I dream of a world where all people are brothers and sisters, recognizing each other as children of God. Social friendship is the recognition that all people, regardless of background, share a common dignity and a common destiny.

THE GOOD SAMARITAN
The parable of the Good Samaritan shows us how to build a fraternal world. The Samaritan saw a wounded man, had compassion, and stopped to help. He did not ask where the man was from or what he believed. He simply saw a human being in need.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus tells us: "Love your neighbor as yourself." The neighbor is anyone in need. We are called to build bridges, not walls. We are all brothers and sisters in God's family.
