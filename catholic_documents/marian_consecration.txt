WHAT IS MARIAN CONSECRATION?
Marian consecration is entrusting oneself entirely to Mary, so that she may lead us more perfectly to Jesus. As St. Louis de Montfort taught, "To Jesus through Mary."

ST. LOUIS DE MONTFORT (1673-1716)
St. Louis de Montfort wrote "True Devotion to Mary" and "The Secret of Mary." He taught that consecration to Mary is the surest, easiest, shortest, and most perfect way to union with Christ.

ST. JOHN PAUL II'S MOTTO
"Totus Tuus" (Totally Yours). St. John Paul II consecrated himself to Mary and encouraged all the faithful to do the same.

THE FIVE FIRST SATURDAYS
Our Lady of Fatima requested the practice of the Five First Saturdays: Confession, Communion, Rosary, and 15 minutes of meditation on the mysteries of the Rosary, offered in reparation for sins against her Immaculate Heart.

THE BROWN SCAPULAR
Our Lady of Mount Carmel gave the Brown Scapular to St. Simon Stock as a sign of her protection. Wearing it signifies consecration to Mary.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Behold, your mother." Entrusting ourselves to Mary is not putting her above Jesus but allowing her to lead us to him as she led him in his earthly life.
