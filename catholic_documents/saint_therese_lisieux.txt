SOURCE: St. Therese of Lisieux (1873-1897)
Doctor of the Church, The Little Flower

KEY TEACHINGS

THE LITTLE WAY
Therese discovered that holiness is not about doing great things, but doing small things with great love. She called this her "little way" of spiritual childhood.

CONFIDENCE IN GOD'S MERCY
She taught that we should approach God with complete trust, like a child approaching a loving parent. Her famous prayer: "I will spend my heaven doing good on earth."

SIMPLICITY AND HUMILITY
Therese never sought greatness. She found her vocation in the heart of the Church: love. She said, "In the heart of the Church, my vocation is love."

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Love proves itself by deeds, so how am I to show my love? Great deeds are forbidden me. The only way I can prove my love is by scattering flowers, and these flowers are every little sacrifice."
"Miss no single opportunity of making some small sacrifice, here by a smiling look, there by a kindly word."
"Everything is a grace."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Unless you become like little children, you will not enter the kingdom of heaven." St. Therese shows us that childlike trust in God's love is the path to holiness.

Source: Story of a Soul (Autobiography of St. Therese)
