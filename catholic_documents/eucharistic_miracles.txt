KEY EUCHARISTIC MIRACLES

LANCIANO, ITALY (8th Century)
A Basilian monk doubted the Real Presence. During the consecration, the bread turned into flesh and the wine turned into blood. Scientific analysis confirmed it is human heart tissue, type AB blood.

BOLSENA-ORVIETO (1263)
A priest doubted the Real Presence. During Mass, blood began to seep from the consecrated Host. The blood-stained corporal is still venerated in Orvieto.

SANTAREM, PORTUGAL (13th Century)
A woman, fearing her husband would find the consecrated Host, left it in a chest. It began to glow and exuded blood. It remains incorrupt today.

BUENOS AIRES (1996)
A Host was found discarded in a candle holder. It began to turn into bloody tissue. Analysis by a cardiologist confirmed it is human heart muscle with signs of severe trauma consistent with crucifixion.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "This is my body, given up for you." Eucharistic miracles confirm that what Jesus said at the Last Supper is true: the Eucharist is truly his Body and Blood, not merely a symbol.
