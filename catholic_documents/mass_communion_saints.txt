SOURCE: The Mass and the Communion of Saints

THE HEAVENLY LITURGY
- The Mass joins us to the worship of heaven (Revelation 4-5)
- Angels and saints adore God eternally
- We join their song: "Holy, Holy, Holy"
- The veil between heaven and earth is thin at Mass

THE SAINTS AT MASS
- The saints are present, interceding for us
- The Eucharistic Prayer names saints
- We are surrounded by a cloud of witnesses
- The Church triumphant, militant, and suffering are united

PRAYING TO THE SAINTS IN THE MASS
- The saints are invoked in the Eucharistic Prayer
- We ask their intercession
- Their feast days celebrate their witness
- They pray with us and for us

MASSES FOR THE FAITHFUL DEPARTED
- Masses can be offered for the dead
- The souls in purgatory benefit from our prayers
- All Souls' Day (November 2) is dedicated to the departed
- The Mass is the greatest prayer for the dead

THE MASS FOR THE LIVING
- Masses for the living are offered for their intentions
- We can request Mass intentions
- The fruits of the Mass are applied to the intention
- The Mass benefits the whole Church

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "I am the God of the living." At Mass, we join with all the saints and angels, the living and the dead, in one great act of worship.

Source: Catechism 1370-1372; Liturgy of the Hours
