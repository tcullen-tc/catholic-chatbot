THE DEAD SEA SCROLLS
The Dead Sea Scrolls are among the most significant archaeological discoveries of the 20th century. Discovered between 1947 and 1956 in the Qumran Caves near the Dead Sea, these scrolls contain the oldest known copies of the Hebrew Bible. They date from approximately 250 BC to AD 68.

CODEX SINAITICUS
Codex Sinaiticus is one of the most important manuscripts of the Bible. Dating to the mid-4th century, it contains the oldest complete copy of the New Testament. The manuscript was discovered at St. Catherine's Monastery on Mount Sinai in 1844.

CODEX VATICANUS
Codex Vaticanus is another important 4th-century manuscript housed in the Vatican Library. Along with Codex Sinaiticus, it is considered one of the most reliable witnesses to the original Greek text of the Bible.

THE GUTENBERG BIBLE
Printed in Mainz, Germany, around 1455 by Johannes Gutenberg, this was the first major book printed using movable type in the West. Approximately 48 copies survive today.

THE KING JAMES VERSION
Commissioned in 1604 by King James I of England, the King James Version (KJV) was first published in 1611. It became the standard English Bible for centuries and influenced English literature and language profoundly.
