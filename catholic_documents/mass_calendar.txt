THE LITURGICAL YEAR
- Advent: Preparation for Christmas (4 weeks)
- Christmas: Birth of the Lord (December 25 - Baptism of the Lord)
- Ordinary Time: Weeks after Christmas and after Easter
- Lent: Preparation for Easter (40 days)
- Easter Triduum: Holy Thursday, Good Friday, Holy Saturday, Easter Vigil
- Easter Season: 50 days until Pentecost
- Ordinary Time: Continues until Advent

SOLEMNITIES, FEASTS, AND MEMORIALS
- Solemnities: Highest rank (Easter, Christmas, Mary, major saints)
- Feasts: Second rank (apostles, evangelists, important events)
- Memorials: Third rank (saints)
- Optional Memorials: May be celebrated or omitted

THE SUNDAY LECTIONARY
- Three-year cycle (A, B, C)
- Year A: Gospel of Matthew
- Year B: Gospel of Mark
- Year C: Gospel of Luke
- Gospel of John used during Lent and Easter

DAILY MASS READINGS
- Two-year cycle (I and II)
- First reading from Old Testament or Acts
- Responsorial Psalm
- Gospel from the day
- Readings for saints' feasts

THE SEASONS IN COLOR
- Advent: Purple (preparation, penance)
- Christmas: White (joy, celebration)
- Ordinary Time: Green (growth, hope)
- Lent: Purple (penance, conversion)
- Easter: White/Gold (joy, resurrection)
- Martyrs: Red
- Saints: White

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The liturgical calendar brings us through the entire life of Christ each year. It is a school of faith, teaching us to live in the mystery of salvation.

Source: General Norms for the Liturgical Year; Roman Missal
