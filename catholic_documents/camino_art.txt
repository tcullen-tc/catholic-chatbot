ROMANESQUE CHURCHES
- Santa María de Eunate (Navarre): Octagonal church, symbol of pilgrimage
- San Martín de Frómista (Palencia): Perfect example of Romanesque
- Santo Domingo de la Calzada (La Rioja): Romanesque with Gothic additions
- The Pórtico de la Gloria (Santiago): Masterpiece of Romanesque sculpture

GOTHIC CATHEDRALS
- Burgos Cathedral: French Gothic, UNESCO site
- León Cathedral: "Pulchra Leonina" with magnificent stained glass
- Astorga Cathedral: Gothic with later additions

CRUCIFIXES AND IMAGES
- Cristo de Burgos (Burgos): Miraculous crucifix
- Virgen del Camino (León): Patroness of the Camino
- Santiago Peregrino: Image of St. James as a pilgrim
- Our Lady of the Pillar (Zaragoza): Connection to the Camino

THE SCALLOP SHELL IN ART
- Found on churches, fountains, and monuments along the way
- Symbol of hospitality and pilgrimage
- Often carved into medieval tombstones
- Marks the direction of the Camino

CROSSES AND WAYMARKERS
- Cruz de Ferro (Iron Cross): The highest point on the French Way
- Pilgrims leave stones, representing burdens left behind
- Yellow arrows: Modern markers painted in 1987
- Milestones: Medieval markers with distance to Santiago

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
These churches and works of art were built by pilgrims for pilgrims. They are signs of faith left by those who walked before us.
