SOURCE: The Eucharistic Prayers of the Mass

EUCHARISTIC PRAYER I (The Roman Canon)
- The oldest prayer, used since the 4th century
- Contains the names of saints and martyrs
- Used on solemnities and special occasions
- Rich in ancient Roman tradition

EUCHARISTIC PRAYER II
- Based on the ancient prayer of St. Hippolytus (c. 215)
- Shorter and more direct
- Suitable for weekdays
- Emphasizes the work of the Holy Spirit

EUCHARISTIC PRAYER III
- Balanced and comprehensive
- Used on Sundays and feast days
- Includes intercessions for the living and the dead
- Highlights the paschal mystery

EUCHARISTIC PRAYER IV
- Longer, with a detailed history of salvation
- Traces God's plan from creation to redemption
- No proper preface (used with its own)
- Suitable for Sundays in Ordinary Time

EUCHARISTIC PRAYERS FOR RECONCILIATION (I and II)
- Focus on God's mercy
- Used during Lent and for reconciliation Masses
- Emphasize conversion and forgiveness

EUCHARISTIC PRAYERS FOR VARIOUS NEEDS
- For use with special intentions
- Include prayers for the Church, for unity, for vocations
- Four options

WHAT EACH PRAYER TEACHES
- We thank God for creation and salvation
- We invoke the Holy Spirit (epiclesis)
- We recall the words of Jesus (institution narrative)
- We offer the sacrifice
- We intercede for the living and the dead

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
In every Eucharistic Prayer, we do what Jesus commanded: "Do this in memory of me." Each prayer is a variation on the same sacred action.
