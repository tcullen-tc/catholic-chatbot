SOURCE: St. Clare of Assisi (1194-1253)
Foundress of the Poor Clares

KEY TEACHINGS

CONTEMPLATIVE PRAYER
Clare left wealth to follow St. Francis and founded a contemplative order. She taught that prayer is the source of strength for all apostolic work.

TRUST IN THE EUCHARIST
When armies attacked her convent, Clare held up the Blessed Sacrament and prayed. The attackers fled. She showed that trust in the Eucharist brings protection and peace.

POOR IN SPIRIT
Clare lived radical poverty. She taught that detachment from material things frees us to love God fully.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Love God, serve God: everything is in that."
"Place your mind before the mirror of eternity. Place your soul in the splendor of glory."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." St. Clare shows us that poverty of spirit means trusting God completely and finding our treasure in him alone.

Source: Writings of St. Clare; Rule of St. Clare
