WHAT IS EUCHARISTIC ADORATION?
- Worship of Jesus Christ truly present in the Blessed Sacrament
- The Eucharist is reserved in the tabernacle
- Exposition: The Host is placed in a monstrance for adoration
- Adoration can be private or public

FORMS OF ADORATION
- Visit to the Blessed Sacrament: Spending time in prayer before the tabernacle
- Exposition: The Host is displayed for adoration
- Holy Hour: An hour of prayer before the Blessed Sacrament
- Perpetual Adoration: Continuous adoration (day and night)
- Benediction: Blessing with the Blessed Sacrament

WHY ADORE THE EUCHARIST?
- Jesus is truly present
- Adoration deepens our union with Christ
- It is a response of love and gratitude
- It bears fruit in the Church and world

THE PRACTICE OF ADORATION
- Begin with the Sign of the Cross
- Sit, kneel, or pray silently
- Read Scripture, pray the Rosary, or simply be still
- Bring your needs and the needs of others
- End with thanksgiving

POPE FRANCIS ON ADORATION
"In the presence of the Eucharist, we learn to listen to the Lord. Adoration is the way to receive his love and to respond to it."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus in the Garden asked, "Could you not watch with me one hour?" Eucharistic adoration is our answer to that question, spending time with the One who loves us.
