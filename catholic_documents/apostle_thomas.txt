SOURCE: St. Thomas - The Doubting Apostle

KEY FACTS
- Also called Didymus (meaning "twin")
- Known for doubting the Resurrection
- Apostle to India
- Patron saint of architects, builders, and India
- Martyrdom: Pierced with a spear in India (c. 72 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- The Doubt: Thomas says, "Unless I see the nail marks in his hands and put my finger where the nails were, I will not believe" (John 20:25)
- The Appearance: Jesus appears and invites Thomas to touch his wounds. Thomas responds, "My Lord and my God!" (John 20:27-28)
- Jesus' Teaching: "Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have believed." (John 20:29)
- Willing to Die: When Jesus decides to go to Lazarus, Thomas says, "Let us also go, that we may die with him." (John 11:16)
- The Way, Truth, Life: Thomas asks, "Lord, we don't know where you are going. How can we know the way?" Jesus responds, "I am the way, the truth, and the life." (John 14:5-6)

TEACHINGS
Thomas doubted, but his doubt led to the greatest confession of faith: "My Lord and my God!" His journey from doubt to belief encourages those who struggle with faith.

TRADITION IN INDIA
Thomas is believed to have traveled to India, where he established Christian communities. The St. Thomas Christians in India trace their origins to him.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"My Lord and my God!" (John 20:28)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Doubt is not the opposite of faith. Thomas doubted, but he stayed with the community. Jesus met him in his doubt and brought him to belief.
