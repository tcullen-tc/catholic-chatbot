SOURCE: St. Maximilian Kolbe (1894-1941)
Martyr of Charity

KEY TEACHINGS

LOVE TO THE END
Kolbe was a Franciscan friar who volunteered to die in place of a stranger at Auschwitz. He showed that love is willing to sacrifice everything, even life itself.

IMMACULATE CONCEPTION
Kolbe had deep devotion to Mary. He called her the "Immaculata" and spread devotion to her throughout his life. He believed she would lead all to Christ.

NO ROOM FOR HATRED
Even in Auschwitz, Kolbe showed no hatred toward his captors. He forgave them and prayed for them. His love overcame the evil around him.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Only love creates. Hatred destroys."
"The most deadly poison of our times is indifference."
"For Jesus Christ, I am ready to suffer still more."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Greater love has no one than this: to lay down one's life for one's friends." St. Maximilian Kolbe lived this commandment to its fullest. He shows us that love conquers even death.
