SOURCE: The Mysteries of the Rosary

JOYFUL MYSTERIES (Mondays and Saturdays)
1. The Annunciation: The angel Gabriel announces to Mary that she will be the Mother of God.
2. The Visitation: Mary visits her cousin Elizabeth.
3. The Nativity: Jesus is born in Bethlehem.
4. The Presentation: Jesus is presented in the Temple.
5. The Finding in the Temple: Jesus is found teaching in the Temple.

SORROWFUL MYSTERIES (Tuesdays and Fridays)
1. The Agony in the Garden: Jesus prays in Gethsemane.
2. The Scourging at the Pillar: Jesus is scourged.
3. The Crowning with Thorns: Jesus is crowned with thorns.
4. The Carrying of the Cross: Jesus carries his cross to Calvary.
5. The Crucifixion: Jesus dies on the cross.

GLORIOUS MYSTERIES (Wednesdays and Sundays)
1. The Resurrection: Jesus rises from the dead.
2. The Ascension: Jesus ascends into heaven.
3. The Descent of the Holy Spirit: The Holy Spirit descends at Pentecost.
4. The Assumption: Mary is taken body and soul into heaven.
5. The Coronation: Mary is crowned Queen of Heaven.

LUMINOUS MYSTERIES (Thursdays)
1. The Baptism of Jesus: Jesus is baptized in the Jordan.
2. The Wedding at Cana: Jesus turns water into wine at Mary's request.
3. The Proclamation of the Kingdom: Jesus preaches the Gospel.
4. The Transfiguration: Jesus is transfigured on Mount Tabor.
5. The Institution of the Eucharist: Jesus gives us his Body and Blood.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Praying the Rosary is meditating on the life of Jesus through the eyes of his mother. Each mystery draws us deeper into the Gospel.

Source: St. John Paul II, Rosarium Virginis Mariae; Tradition
