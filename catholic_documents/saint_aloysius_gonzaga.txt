SOURCE: St. Aloysius Gonzaga (1568-1591)
Patron of Youth

KEY TEACHINGS

RENOUNCING THE WORLD
Aloysius was born into a wealthy noble family. He renounced his inheritance to become a Jesuit. He chose poverty and service over power and luxury.

SERVING THE SICK
During a plague in Rome, Aloysius cared for the sick. He caught the plague and died at age 23. He gave his life serving others.

PURITY AND JOY
Aloysius was known for his purity, his joy, and his deep prayer life. He said that he would rather die than commit even the smallest sin.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"I am a twisted piece of iron, and I entered religion to be straightened."
"It is better to be the child of God than the king of the whole world."
"I would rather die than sin."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "What does it profit a man to gain the whole world and forfeit his soul?" St. Aloysius Gonzaga shows us that nothing is worth more than following Christ.
