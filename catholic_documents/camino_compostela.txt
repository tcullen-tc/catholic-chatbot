THE COMPOSTELA
The Compostela is the certificate of completion given to pilgrims who:
- Walk at least 100 km (62 miles)
- Cycle or ride horseback at least 200 km
- Have Christian motivation (can be spiritual, religious, or cultural)
- Receive the certificate at the Pilgrim's Office in Santiago

THE PILGRIM'S MASS
- Held daily at the Cathedral of Santiago
- Special blessing for pilgrims
- The Botafumeiro (giant incense burner) swings during the Mass
- The largest Botafumeiro weighs 53 kg (117 lbs) and swings at 68 km/h

THE PILGRIM'S PASSPORT (Credencial)
- Document for collecting stamps (sellos) along the way
- Required to receive the Compostela
- Stamps from churches, hostels, bars, town halls
- Proves the pilgrim's journey

THE SCALLOP SHELL
- Symbol of St. James
- Worn on backpacks, hats, or walking sticks
- Represents the many paths converging at Santiago
- Used as a water scoop by medieval pilgrims

THE PILGRIM'S STAFF (Bordón)
- Traditional walking stick
- Has a hook for carrying a gourd
- Symbol of the pilgrim's journey
- Represents the staff of pilgrims since biblical times

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The journey is as important as the destination. Every stamp, every step, every kindness received along the way is part of the pilgrimage.

Source: Cathedral of Santiago; Pilgrim's Office
