SOURCE: St. Tarcisius (3rd Century)
Martyr of the Eucharist

KEY TEACHINGS

THE EUCHARIST ABOVE ALL
Tarcisius was a young acolyte in Rome during persecution. He was tasked with bringing the Eucharist to imprisoned Christians. When attacked by pagans, he protected the Eucharist with his life.

MARTYRED FOR THE EUCHARIST
He was beaten to death rather than let the Sacred Host fall into pagan hands. He died holding the Eucharist close to his heart.

PATRON OF FIRST COMMUNICANTS
Tarcisius is the patron saint of first communicants and altar servers. His story teaches us the precious value of the Eucharist.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"He chose to die rather than give up the Body of Christ to ravening dogs."
"Nothing is more precious than the Body of Christ."

HIS LEGACY
St. Pope Damasus wrote an epitaph for Tarcisius, praising him for giving his life for the Eucharist. He is remembered as a model of courage for young people.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Whoever loses his life for my sake will find it." St. Tarcisius shows us that the Eucharist is worth everything. He gave his life to protect the Body of Christ.

Source: Acts of the Martyrs; St. Pope Damasus
