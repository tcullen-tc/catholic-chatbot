SOURCE: St. John Chrysostom (c. 347-407)
Homilies on the Gospels

KEY TEACHINGS

THE GOSPEL IS GOOD NEWS
Chrysostom was known as "golden-mouthed" for his powerful preaching. He proclaimed that the Gospel is not a burden but good news of God's love and mercy.

PRACTICAL FAITH
He taught that faith must be lived. It is not enough to believe; we must act. Charity, forgiveness, and mercy are the fruits of true faith.

SCRIPTURE FOR EVERYONE
Chrysostom encouraged all Christians, not just monks and clergy, to read and study Scripture. The Bible is the treasury of divine wisdom for all.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"No one is excluded from this calling: neither the sinner, nor the tax collector, nor the impure. God invites everyone."
"The road to hell is paved with the skulls of bishops."
"Not to share our own wealth with the poor is theft from the poor and deprivation of their means of life."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Go into all the world and preach the Gospel to every creature." St. John Chrysostom shows us that the Gospel is for everyone and must be lived in daily life.
