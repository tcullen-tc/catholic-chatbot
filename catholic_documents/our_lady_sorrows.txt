THE SEVEN SORROWS OF MARY

1. The Prophecy of Simeon (Luke 2:34-35)
Simeon tells Mary that a sword will pierce her soul, foretelling her share in Christ's suffering.

2. The Flight into Egypt (Matthew 2:13-15)
Mary flees with Joseph and the infant Jesus to escape Herod's murderous soldiers.

3. The Loss of Jesus in the Temple (Luke 2:41-52)
Mary and Joseph search for Jesus for three days, anxious and sorrowful.

4. Mary Meets Jesus on the Way to Calvary
Tradition holds that Mary met Jesus as he carried his cross, their eyes meeting in sorrow and love.

5. The Crucifixion (John 19:25-27)
Mary stands at the foot of the cross, witnessing her Son's death.

6. Mary Receives the Body of Jesus
The Pietà: Mary holds the lifeless body of her Son, a moment of profound grief.

7. The Burial of Jesus
Mary watches as her Son's body is laid in the tomb.

DEVOTION TO OUR LADY OF SORROWS
The Feast of Our Lady of Sorrows is celebrated on September 15. The Seven Sorrows Rosary consists of seven groups of seven Hail Marys, meditating on each sorrow.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Mary's sorrows unite her with her Son's passion. She shows us how to suffer with hope, trusting that sorrow will be transformed into joy at the Resurrection.
