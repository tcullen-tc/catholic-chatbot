SOURCE: St. Edith Stein (1891-1942)
Martyr, Co-Patron of Europe

KEY TEACHINGS

TRUTH SEEKER
Edith was a brilliant Jewish philosopher who became Catholic after reading St. Teresa of Avila. She became a Carmelite nun and continued to write about philosophy and spirituality.

SUFFERING WITH CHRIST
She saw her suffering as sharing in Christ's cross. In Auschwitz, she helped other prisoners and remained at peace, united with her crucified Lord.

UNITY OF JEWISH AND CHRISTIAN HERITAGE
Edith never rejected her Jewish roots. She saw Christianity as the fulfillment of Judaism and prayed for reconciliation between Christians and Jews.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Those who remain silent are responsible."
"Do not accept anything as love that is without truth."
"The cross is the path to the resurrection. Do not shrink from it."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "If any man would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me." St. Edith Stein shows us that suffering, when united with Christ, becomes a path to resurrection and hope.

Source: Writings of St. Edith Stein; Lives of the Saints
