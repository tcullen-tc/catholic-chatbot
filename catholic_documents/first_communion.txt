THE MEANING OF FIRST COMMUNION
First Holy Communion is a child's first reception of the Eucharist. It is a major milestone in the life of a Catholic, usually celebrated around age 7-8.

PREPARATION
Children prepare through catechesis about:
- The Real Presence of Jesus in the Eucharist
- The importance of the state of grace
- How to receive Communion reverently

WHAT TO TEACH CHILDREN
- Jesus is truly present in the Eucharist
- Receiving Communion is receiving Jesus himself
- We prepare by prayer, fasting, and confession
- We thank Jesus after Communion

ST. JOHN VIANNEY ON FIRST COMMUNION
"There is nothing so great as the Eucharist. If God had something more precious, he would have given it to us."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Let the little children come to me." First Communion is a precious moment when a child meets Jesus in the Eucharist for the first time.
