KEY PRINCIPLES OF CATHOLIC SOCIAL TEACHING

HUMAN DIGNITY
Every person is made in the image of God. Human life is sacred from conception to natural death. This is the foundation of all Catholic social teaching.

COMMON GOOD
The social conditions that allow people to reach their full human potential and realize their dignity. The common good is not the sum of individual goods but the good of all people.

SUBSIDIARITY
Decisions should be made at the most local level possible. Higher authorities should only intervene when necessary to support lower levels.

SOLIDARITY
We are one human family. Solidarity is the virtue that recognizes we are our brothers' and sisters' keepers.

OPTION FOR THE POOR
The needs of the poor and vulnerable are the priority. How we treat the least among us is the measure of our faithfulness.

CARE FOR CREATION
We are stewards of God's creation. The earth and its resources are a gift to be cared for, not exploited.

Sources: Catechism of the Catholic Church; Compendium of the Social Doctrine of the Church
