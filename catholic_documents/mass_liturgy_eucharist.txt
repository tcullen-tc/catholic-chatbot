SOURCE: The Liturgy of the Eucharist

PREPARATION OF THE GIFTS
- Presentation of bread and wine
- The priest prepares the altar
- Prayer over the offerings
- Collection for the Church and the poor

THE EUCHARISTIC PRAYER (The heart of the Mass)
- Preface: We give thanks and praise
- Sanctus: "Holy, Holy, Holy Lord"
- Epiclesis: Invocation of the Holy Spirit to transform the gifts
- Institution Narrative: Words of Jesus at the Last Supper
- Consecration: The bread and wine become the Body and Blood of Christ
- Anamnesis: Memorial of Christ's death and resurrection
- Intercessions: Prayers for the Church, the living, and the dead
- Doxology: "Through him, with him, in him..."
- Great Amen: Our assent

THE CONSECRATION
- The priest speaks in the person of Christ
- "This is my Body... This is my Blood"
- Transubstantiation: The substance of bread and wine becomes Christ
- The bells ring to mark this sacred moment

THE COMMUNION RITE
- Our Father: The Lord's Prayer
- Sign of Peace: Offering peace to one another
- Lamb of God: Invocation for mercy
- Fraction: Breaking of the Bread
- Communion: Receiving the Body and Blood of Christ

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Take, eat; this is my body... Drink of it, all of you; for this is my blood of the covenant." At the Consecration, we enter the Upper Room and Calvary at once.

Source: General Instruction of the Roman Missal; Catechism
