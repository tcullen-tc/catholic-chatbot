HISTORY
The Carmelite order traces its origins to the prophets Elijah and Elisha on Mount Carmel in the Holy Land. In the 13th century, the Carmelites migrated to Europe, where Our Lady appeared to St. Simon Stock.

THE BROWN SCAPULAR (1251)
Our Lady appeared to St. Simon Stock, General of the Carmelite Order, and gave him the Brown Scapular with the promise: "Whoever dies wearing this scapular shall not suffer eternal fire." The scapular is a sign of consecration to Mary and a reminder of her protection.

PROMISES OF THE SCAPULAR
- Protection in life and in death
- The "Sabbatine Privilege": Mary promised to intercede for those who wear the scapular, especially on Saturdays
- A sign of belonging to Mary

HOW TO WEAR THE SCAPULAR
The Brown Scapular is to be worn over the shoulders, with one square on the chest and one on the back. It must be invested by a priest. After investment, it is a perpetual commitment.

WHAT THE CHURCH TEACHES
The scapular is not a "magic charm" but a sign of faith and devotion. It calls the wearer to live a life of prayer, chastity, and service.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus honored his mother and gives us signs of her love. The Brown Scapular is a reminder that we are under Mary's protection and are called to live as her children.
