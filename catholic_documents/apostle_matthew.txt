SOURCE: St. Matthew - Tax Collector Turned Evangelist

KEY FACTS
- Original name: Levi
- Tax collector (publican) before following Jesus
- Author of the Gospel of Matthew
- Patron saint of tax collectors, accountants, and bankers
- Martyrdom: Martyred in Ethiopia or Persia (c. 60-70 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- The Call: Jesus calls Matthew from his tax booth: "Follow me" (Matthew 9:9)
- The Banquet: Matthew hosts a feast for Jesus, inviting other tax collectors and sinners (Matthew 9:10-13)
- Jesus' Response to Criticism: "I desire mercy, not sacrifice. I did not come to call the righteous, but sinners." (Matthew 9:13)
- Gospel Writer: Matthew's Gospel is the most Jewish, emphasizing Jesus as the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecy

TEACHINGS
Matthew shows us that no one is beyond God's mercy. Tax collectors were despised in Jesus' time, yet Matthew became an apostle and evangelist.

MATTHEW'S GOSPEL THEMES
- The Kingdom of Heaven (used 33 times)
- Jesus as the New Moses
- The Five Discourses (Sermon on the Mount, Mission Discourse, Parables, Church Order, Eschatological Discourse)
- The Great Commission: "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:19)

FAMOUS QUOTES FROM JESUS IN MATTHEW
"Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven." (Matthew 5:3)
"You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my Church." (Matthew 16:18)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus sees beyond our past sins. He called a tax collector to be his apostle. No one is too far from his mercy.
