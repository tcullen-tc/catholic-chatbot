SOURCE: St. Jerome (c. 347-420)
On Sacred Scripture

KEY TEACHINGS

IGNORANCE OF SCRIPTURE IS IGNORANCE OF CHRIST
Jerome famously said, "Ignorance of Scripture is ignorance of Christ." He dedicated his life to translating and explaining the Bible.

THE VULGATE TRANSLATION
Jerome translated the Bible into Latin, creating the Vulgate, which became the standard Bible of the Western Church for over a thousand years.

STUDY AND PRAYER
For Jerome, studying Scripture was inseparable from prayer. The Word of God is alive and speaks to those who approach it with faith.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Read the divine Scriptures constantly; never let the sacred volume fall from your hand."
"The Scriptures are shallow enough for a child to wade in, yet deep enough for an elephant to swim."
"When we pray, we speak to God; when we read Scripture, God speaks to us."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God." St. Jerome calls us to know Scripture, for in it we encounter Christ.
