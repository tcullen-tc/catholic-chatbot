SOURCE: St. Francis de Sales (1567-1622)
Doctor of the Church

KEY TEACHINGS

GENTLENESS AND PATIENCE
St. Francis is known for his gentle approach to spirituality. He taught that "a spoonful of honey attracts more flies than a barrel of vinegar." Gentleness is more effective than harshness in bringing souls to God.

INTRODUCTION TO THE DEVOUT LIFE
His masterpiece, "Introduction to the Devout Life," was written for ordinary people living in the world. He taught that holiness is not only for monks and nuns but for everyone in every state of life.

GOD'S LOVE
Francis emphasized that we should serve God out of love, not fear. "Do not fear what may happen tomorrow. The same loving Father who cares for you today will care for you tomorrow."

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Have patience with all things, but first of all with yourself."
"Nothing is so strong as gentleness, nothing so gentle as real strength."
"Do not wish to be anything but what you are, and try to be that perfectly."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart." St. Francis de Sales shows us that true holiness is found in gentleness, patience, and trusting God's love.

Source: Introduction to the Devout Life; Treatise on the Love of God
