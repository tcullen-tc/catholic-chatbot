SOURCE: Theology of the Mass

THE MASS AS SACRIFICE
- The Mass is the same sacrifice as Calvary, offered sacramentally
- Not a new sacrifice but the same one made present
- The priest acts in the person of Christ (in persona Christi)
- The faithful unite their offering with Christ's

THE MASS AS MEMORIAL (Anamnesis)
- Not merely a remembrance but a making present
- The biblical understanding of memorial (zikkaron)
- Past events become present in liturgical celebration
- We enter the Upper Room and Calvary

THE MASS AS BANQUET
- The Eucharist is a meal, not merely a sacrifice
- The Lord's Supper, the Wedding Feast of the Lamb
- Communion is intimate union with Christ
- Foretaste of the heavenly banquet

TRANSUBSTANTIATION
- The substance of bread and wine becomes the Body and Blood of Christ
- The accidents (appearance) remain
- Defined by the Council of Trent (1551)
- Affirmed by Vatican II

THE ROLE OF THE HOLY SPIRIT (Epiclesis)
- The Spirit is invoked to transform the gifts
- The Spirit makes Christ present
- The Spirit unites the faithful
- The Mass is Trinitarian

THE MASS AND THE CHURCH
- The Mass makes the Church
- The Church celebrates the Mass
- Active participation is a right and duty
- The whole Church joins in heaven and on earth

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus taught that his flesh is true food and his blood true drink. The theology of the Mass explains how this gift is given to us until he comes again.

Source: Catechism of the Catholic Church 1322-1419; Council of Trent
