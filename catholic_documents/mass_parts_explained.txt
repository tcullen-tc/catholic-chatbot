SOURCE: Parts of the Mass Explained

THE SIGN OF THE CROSS
- We begin and end in the name of the Trinity
- Reminds us of our baptism
- Prays "In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit"

THE PENITENTIAL ACT
- "I confess to Almighty God..."
- Acknowledges our sins
- Seeks God's mercy
- Prepares us to hear God's Word

THE GLORIA
- Ancient hymn of praise
- Sung on Sundays and solemnities
- Proclaims the glory of the Trinity

THE READINGS
- God speaks to us through Scripture
- Three readings on Sundays
- A response (Psalm) between them

THE HOMILY
- Explains the readings
- Applies them to daily life
- Proclaims the Gospel

THE CREED
- Summary of Catholic faith
- Nicene Creed from the Council of Nicaea (325)
- Profession of faith together

THE SANCTUS
- "Holy, Holy, Holy Lord"
- Joins the angels in heaven
- Leads into the Eucharistic Prayer

THE OUR FATHER
- The prayer Jesus taught
- Prayed before Communion
- Expresses our unity as God's children

THE SIGN OF PEACE
- Offer peace to those around us
- Reflects Jesus' command to be reconciled
- Prepares us for Communion

THE LAMB OF GOD
- "Agnus Dei"
- Invokes Christ the Lamb of God
- Begins the Communion rite

THE FINAL BLESSING
- The priest blesses us
- We are sent forth to live the Mass
- "Go in peace, glorifying the Lord"

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Every part of the Mass has meaning. Learn the parts, enter into them, and let the Mass shape your life.

Source: General Instruction; Catechism
