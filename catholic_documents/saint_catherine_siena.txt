SOURCE: St. Catherine of Siena (1347-1380)
Doctor of the Church

KEY TEACHINGS

THE DIALOGUE
St. Catherine's great work, The Dialogue, is a mystical conversation between God and a soul seeking union with him. It covers divine providence, obedience, prayer, and the love of neighbor.

TRUTH AND LOVE
Catherine was known for her fearless speaking of truth, even to popes and political leaders. She famously urged Pope Gregory XI to return to Rome from Avignon. She taught that truth must be spoken with love.

BLOOD OF CHRIST
Catherine had a deep devotion to the Precious Blood of Christ. She saw the Eucharist as the source of all grace and the means by which souls are united to God.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Be who God meant you to be and you will set the world on fire."
"Proclaim the truth and do not be silent through fear."
"All the way to heaven is heaven, because Jesus said, 'I am the way.'"

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "I am the way, and the truth, and the life." St. Catherine shows us that following Christ means speaking truth with love and living fully who God created us to be.

Sources: The Dialogue of St. Catherine of Siena; St. Catherine's Letters
