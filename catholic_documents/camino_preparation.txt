SOURCE: Preparing for the Camino de Santiago

SPIRITUAL PREPARATION
- Pray daily for your intention
- Practice detachment from comforts
- Read Scripture, especially the Gospels
- Study the life of St. James
- Make a pilgrimage of the heart before the journey of the feet

PHYSICAL PREPARATION
- Train by walking 10-15 km daily for several months
- Break in boots thoroughly before starting
- Practice carrying your full pack weight
- Build endurance for 20-30 km days
- Learn to listen to your body

PRACTICAL PREPARATION
- Pack light (under 10% of body weight)
- Essential items: boots, backpack, sleeping bag, rain gear
- Pilgrim's passport (Credencial) from a Camino association
- Travel insurance and emergency contacts
- Learn basic Spanish phrases

PILGRIM'S MOTTO
"Ultreia" (Go forward, press on)
"Et suseia" (And onward, higher)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "No one who puts his hand to the plow and looks back is fit for the kingdom of God." Preparation is important, but the journey itself is where transformation happens.
