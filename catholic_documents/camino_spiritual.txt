A JOURNEY OF CONVERSION
The Camino is not just a hike but a spiritual exercise. Pilgrims walk to:
- Encounter God in silence and solitude
- Seek healing and reconciliation
- Discover their true self
- Offer prayers and penance

WALKING AS PRAYER
- Each step can be a prayer
- The rhythm of walking opens the heart
- The road becomes a chapel
- The backpack becomes a cross

HOSPITALITY (HOSPITALIDAD)
- Pilgrim hospitality is a spiritual work of mercy
- Albergues (hostels) offer shelter to pilgrims
- Volunteers serve as modern-day Good Samaritans
- "Ultreia" - encouragement to go forward

THE FOUR MOVEMENTS OF THE CAMINO
1. Leaving: Letting go of attachments
2. Walking: The journey itself, with its joys and struggles
3. Arriving: Reaching Santiago, encountering St. James
4. Returning: Bringing the graces home to share with others

POPE FRANCIS ON THE CAMINO
"The Camino is a great opportunity to encounter oneself, to encounter God, to encounter others. It is a school of humility and patience."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Come to me, all who labor and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest." The Camino is a way of coming to him, step by step, carrying our burdens, and finding rest in the journey.
