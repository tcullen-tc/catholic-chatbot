SYMBOLS AND THEIR MEANINGS

ST. PETER: Crossed keys (keys of the kingdom), rooster (his denial), upside-down cross (his martyrdom)

ST. ANDREW: X-shaped cross (his martyrdom), fishing net (his trade)

ST. JAMES THE GREATER: Pilgrim's staff and scallop shell (pilgrimage to Santiago), sword (his martyrdom)

ST. JOHN: Chalice with serpent (attempted poisoning), eagle (soaring theology), book (his writings)

ST. MATTHEW: Tax collector's bag, money, angel (symbol of his Gospel)

ST. THOMAS: Carpenter's square (builder), spear (his martyrdom), hand touching wounds (his doubt)

ST. PHILIP: Cross and basket (the feeding of the 5,000), loaves of bread

ST. BARTHOLOMEW: Knife (his flaying), his own skin (his martyrdom)

ST. JAMES THE LESS: Fuller's club (his martyrdom), saw

ST. SIMON THE ZEALOT: Saw (his martyrdom), fish, book

ST. JUDE THADDEUS: Boat (missionary journeys), club (his martyrdom), medallion with face of Christ

ST. MATTHIAS: Axe (his martyrdom), lance

ST. PAUL: Sword (his martyrdom), book (his writings), open scroll

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Each symbol tells a story of faithfulness. Some apostles died by the sword, some by the cross, some by the saw. They all gave their lives for Christ.
