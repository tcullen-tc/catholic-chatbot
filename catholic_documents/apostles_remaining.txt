SOURCE: The Remaining Apostles

ST. JAMES THE LESS (Son of Alphaeus)
- Called "the Less" to distinguish him from James the Greater
- Author of the Epistle of James (likely)
- Led the Church in Jerusalem after Peter's departure
- Martyred by being thrown from the Temple and beaten to death (c. 62 AD)
- Emphasized faith expressed in works: "Faith without works is dead" (James 2:26)

ST. SIMON THE ZEALOT
- Called "the Zealot," possibly meaning he belonged to the Zealot party
- Little is known from Scripture
- Tradition says he preached in Persia and was martyred
- Patron saint of tanners and the Zealot movement

ST. JUDE THADDEUS
- Also called Thaddaeus or Judas (not Iscariot)
- Author of the Epistle of Jude
- Known as the patron of desperate or hopeless cases
- Tradition says he preached in Mesopotamia and Persia
- Martyred with Simon the Zealot
- His epistle encourages believers to "contend for the faith"

ST. MATTHIAS
- Chosen to replace Judas Iscariot after the Ascension (Acts 1:15-26)
- Was with Jesus from his baptism to his ascension
- Apostle to the Gentiles
- Tradition says he preached in Judea and was martyred

ST. PAUL (Apostle to the Gentiles)
- Originally called Saul, a persecutor of Christians
- Converted on the road to Damascus (Acts 9)
- Author of 13 epistles in the New Testament
- Made three missionary journeys across the Mediterranean
- Martyred by beheading in Rome (c. 64-67 AD)
- Known for teaching salvation by grace through faith

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Each apostle was different—fishermen, tax collector, zealot, scholar. Jesus calls people from every background to follow him. All are called to be witnesses.

Source: Gospels; Acts; Epistles; Tradition
