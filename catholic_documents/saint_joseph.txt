SOURCE: St. Joseph (1st Century)
Husband of Mary, Foster Father of Jesus

KEY TEACHINGS

SILENT OBEDIENCE
St. Joseph never speaks in Scripture. He shows us that holiness is often lived in silence, listening to God's voice and acting with quiet faith.

PROTECTOR OF THE HOLY FAMILY
Joseph protected Mary and Jesus. He trusted God even when he didn't understand. He is the patron saint of workers, fathers, and the universal Church.

VIRTUES OF ST. JOSEPH
- Faith: He believed the angel's message
- Obedience: He followed God's will immediately
- Chastity: He lived pure love with Mary
- Diligence: He worked as a carpenter to provide for his family

FAMOUS QUOTES FROM POPES
Pope St. John Paul II: "Joseph is the model of those humble ones that Christianity raises to great destinies."
Pope Francis: "You can do what Joseph did: be silent, trust, and act."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus learned from Joseph how to work, how to pray, and how to trust God. St. Joseph shows us that holiness is found in daily faithfulness, quiet service, and loving protection of those entrusted to us.

Source: Gospel of Matthew; Gospel of Luke; Pope St. John Paul II, Redemptoris Custos
