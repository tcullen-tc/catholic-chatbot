SOURCE: St. Ignatius of Antioch (c. 35-108)
Apostolic Father, Martyr

KEY TEACHINGS

THE EUCHARIST IS THE MEDICINE OF IMMORTALITY
Ignatius was the first to call the Eucharist the "medicine of immortality." He taught that the Eucharist is truly the Body and Blood of Christ.

UNITY WITH THE BISHOP
Ignatius wrote that Christians should be united with their bishop as Christ is united with the Father. He stressed the importance of Church unity.

LONGING FOR MARTYRDOM
On his way to Rome to be martyred, Ignatius wrote letters begging the Romans not to prevent his death. He wanted to be united with Christ completely.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"I am the wheat of God; let me be ground by the teeth of wild beasts, that I may be found the pure bread of Christ."
"Where the bishop is present, there let the congregation gather, just as where Jesus Christ is, there is the Catholic Church."
"Do not have Jesus Christ on your lips and the world in your heart."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "I am the living bread that came down from heaven. Whoever eats this bread will live forever." St. Ignatius of Antioch shows us that the Eucharist is our food for the journey to eternal life.
