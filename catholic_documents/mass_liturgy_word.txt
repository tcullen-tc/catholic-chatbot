STRUCTURE OF THE LITURGY OF THE WORD

ENTRANCE RITES
- Procession: The priest and ministers enter
- Greeting: "The Lord be with you" – "And with your spirit"
- Penitential Act: We acknowledge our sins
- Kyrie: "Lord, have mercy"
- Gloria: Hymn of praise (sung on Sundays and solemnities)
- Collect: Opening prayer

THE READINGS
- First Reading: Usually from the Old Testament (or Acts during Easter)
- Responsorial Psalm: Sung response to God's Word
- Second Reading: From the New Testament letters (Sundays and solemnities)
- Gospel Acclamation: "Alleluia" (except during Lent)
- Gospel Reading: From Matthew, Mark, Luke, or John

THE HOMILY
- The priest or deacon explains the readings
- Applies Scripture to daily life
- Calls the faithful to conversion
- Not a lecture but a pastoral exhortation

THE CREED (Profession of Faith)
- Nicene Creed (Sundays and solemnities)
- Apostles' Creed (sometimes used)
- We profess our faith together

THE PRAYER OF THE FAITHFUL (Universal Prayer)
- We pray for the Church, world leaders, the suffering, and the local community
- "Lord, hear our prayer"

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds from the mouth of God." In the Liturgy of the Word, we are fed by Scripture, which is living and active.

Source: General Instruction of the Roman Missal
