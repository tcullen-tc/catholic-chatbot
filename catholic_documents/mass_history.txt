SOURCE: History of the Mass

THE LAST SUPPER (c. 33 AD)
- Jesus institutes the Eucharist (Matthew 26:26-28; Mark 14:22-24; Luke 22:19-20)
- "Do this in memory of me"
- The first Mass

THE APOSTOLIC AGE (33-100 AD)
- The breaking of bread (Acts 2:42)
- Prayers, hymns, and teaching
- House churches
- Simple structure: readings, homily, Eucharist

THE EARLY CHURCH (100-313 AD)
- Justin Martyr describes the Mass (c. 155 AD)
- Structure: readings, homily, prayers, Eucharist
- Celebrated on Sunday, the Lord's Day
- Under persecution, in catacombs and homes

THE IMPERIAL CHURCH (313-600 AD)
- Constantine legalizes Christianity
- Basilicas built for worship
- Liturgy becomes more elaborate
- Roman Rite develops

THE MIDDLE AGES (600-1500 AD)
- Gregorian chant develops
- Altar moved to the wall (priest faces same direction as people)
- Latin becomes standard
- Elevation of the Host introduced

THE COUNCIL OF TRENT (1545-1563)
- Standardized the Roman Rite
- Catechism explains the Mass
- Defends the Real Presence against Protestant reformers
- Creates the Roman Missal (1570)

VATICAN COUNCIL II (1962-1965)
- Constitution on the Sacred Liturgy (Sacrosanctum Concilium)
- Vernacular languages introduced
- Priest faces the people
- Active participation emphasized
- New Roman Missal (1969, revised 2011)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The form of the Mass has developed over centuries, but the substance remains what Jesus instituted: the sacrifice of his Body and Blood under the forms of bread and wine.

Source: Sacrosanctum Concilium; Roman Missal; History of the Liturgy
