WHAT IS THE CAMINO?
The Camino de Santiago (Way of St. James) is a network of pilgrimage routes across Europe leading to the Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela in Galicia, Spain, where the relics of St. James the Greater are venerated.

HISTORY
- 9th Century: The tomb of St. James is discovered (c. 813-830 AD)
- King Alfonso II builds the first church
- Medieval times: One of the three great pilgrimages (with Rome and Jerusalem)
- Modern revival: Over 350,000 pilgrims annually (2023 record: 446,000+)

THE PILGRIMAGE
- Pilgrims walk, bike, or ride horseback
- Traditional starting points: St. Jean Pied de Port (France) for the French Way
- Compostela: Certificate of completion for walking at least 100 km or cycling 200 km
- The scallop shell is the universal symbol of the Camino

SPIRITUAL SIGNIFICANCE
- Pilgrimage of conversion and penance
- Journey of self-discovery and encounter with God
- Walking as prayer
- Hospitality as a spiritual practice

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "I am the way, the truth, and the life." The Camino is a physical journey that mirrors our spiritual journey to God. Every step can be a prayer.
