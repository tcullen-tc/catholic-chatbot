SOURCE: St. Agnes (c. 291-304)
Virgin and Martyr

KEY TEACHINGS

PURITY AND COURAGE
St. Agnes was only 12 or 13 years old when she was martyred. She refused to marry a pagan and dedicated herself completely to Christ.

NO COMPROMISE
She rejected all attempts to make her renounce her faith. She said, "I am already promised to my Lord. He is my only spouse."

EARLY MARTYR
Agnes was one of the most venerated martyrs of the early Church. Her feast day (January 21) has been celebrated since the 4th century.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"I am already promised to my Lord. He is my only spouse."
"He chose me first, and he shall have me."

HER SYMBOLS
St. Agnes is often depicted with a lamb (symbol of her purity) and a palm branch (symbol of martyrdom).

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Blessed are the pure in heart, for they shall see God." St. Agnes shows us that purity of heart is worth any sacrifice. She chose Christ above all else.

Source: Acts of the Martyrs; St. Ambrose, On Virgins
