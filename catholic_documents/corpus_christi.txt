HISTORY OF THE FEAST
The Feast of Corpus Christi was established in 1264 by Pope Urban IV, inspired by the Eucharistic miracle of Bolsena and the writings of St. Thomas Aquinas.

ST. THOMAS AQUINAS' HYMNS
St. Thomas composed the beautiful hymns for this feast:
- Pange Lingua (which includes the Tantum Ergo)
- Lauda Sion
- Sacris Solemniis (which includes Panis Angelicus)

TRADITIONS
- Eucharistic Processions: The Blessed Sacrament is carried through the streets, showing that Christ walks with his people
- Benediction: The blessing with the Blessed Sacrament
- Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament

WHAT THE FEAST TEACHES
Corpus Christi is a public profession of faith in the Real Presence. It reminds us that the Eucharist is not just for private devotion but for the whole community.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "I am the living bread that came down from heaven." The Feast of Corpus Christi is our joyful response to this gift, proclaiming our faith in his presence among us.
