SOURCE: Early Church Fathers on Mary

ST. IRENAEUS OF LYONS (c. 130-202)
"The knot of Eve's disobedience was loosed by the obedience of Mary. What the virgin Eve had bound in unbelief, the Virgin Mary loosed through faith." (Against Heresies)

ST. EPHREM THE SYRIAN (c. 306-373)
"Mary is the blessed one who was predestined to be the Mother of God. She is the new Eve, the mother of all the living."

ST. ATHANASIUS (c. 296-373)
"Mary is the Mother of God because the Word became flesh in her womb. She is the throne of God, the ark of the covenant."

ST. AMBROSE OF MILAN (c. 340-397)
"Mary was a virgin not only in body but also in mind. She conceived the Lord not by human desire but by divine grace."

ST. JEROME (c. 347-420)
"Mary remained a virgin before, during, and after the birth of Christ. Her virginity is a sign of her total consecration to God."

ST. AUGUSTINE (354-430)
"Mary is more blessed in receiving the faith of Christ than in conceiving the flesh of Christ."

ST. JOHN CHRYSOSTOM (c. 347-407)
"Mary is the Mother of God, and this is the highest honor that can be given to any creature."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The Church Fathers, guided by the Holy Spirit, recognized Mary's unique role from the earliest centuries. Their writings show that devotion to Mary is not a later addition but part of the deposit of faith.
