SOURCE: St. Peter - Prince of the Apostles

KEY FACTS
- Original name: Simon (Hebrew: Shimon)
- Called by Jesus to be a "fisher of men" (Mark 1:16-18)
- Given the name Peter (Greek: Petros, meaning rock) by Jesus (Matthew 16:18)
- First Pope and first Bishop of Rome
- Martyrdom: Crucified upside down in Rome under Nero (c. 64-67 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- Confession of Faith: "You are the Christ, the Son of the living God" (Matthew 16:16)
- Walking on Water: Peter steps out of the boat in faith (Matthew 14:28-31)
- Denial of Jesus: Peter denies knowing Jesus three times (Luke 22:54-62)
- Restoration: Jesus asks Peter three times, "Do you love me?" (John 21:15-19)
- Pentecost: Peter preaches the first sermon; 3,000 convert (Acts 2)

TEACHINGS
Peter learned that faith requires both courage and humility. His failures taught him mercy. His success came from the power of the Holy Spirit, not his own strength.

PAPAL SUCCESSION
Jesus said to Peter: "You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my Church. I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven." (Matthew 16:18-19) This establishes the papacy, which continues through the popes today.

FAMOUS QUOTES FROM PETER
"Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life." (John 6:68)
"Repent and be baptized, every one of you, in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins." (Acts 2:38)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Peter was impulsive, imperfect, and sometimes fearful. But he loved Jesus and was willing to learn. Jesus sees our potential, not just our failures. He calls us to be "rock" for others.

Source: Gospels; Acts of the Apostles; Tradition
