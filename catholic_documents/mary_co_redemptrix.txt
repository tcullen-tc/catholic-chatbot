MARY AS CO-REDEMPTRIX
The title "Co-Redemptrix" does not mean Mary is equal to Christ. Rather, it means she participated uniquely in the work of redemption through her fiat (yes) to God and her union with Christ's suffering. As Eve contributed to the fall, Mary contributed to redemption.

THEOLOGICAL FOUNDATION
- Her fiat: Mary's "yes" opened the way for the Incarnation
- Her union with Christ: She stood at the foot of the cross, offering her Son for the salvation of the world
- Her role in the economy of salvation: She cooperated in a unique way in the work of redemption

MARY AS MEDIATRIX
All graces come through Christ, but Mary intercedes for us as the Mother of Mercy. This is not a separate mediation but a participation in Christ's one mediation.

POPE ST. JOHN PAUL II ON MARY
"The Mother of the Redeemer has a precise place in the plan of salvation. Her role in the economy of salvation is not an obstacle to the unique mediation of Christ but is subordinate to it."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Woman, behold your son." From the cross, he entrusted his mother to us and us to her. She is our mother, always interceding for us.

Source: Catechism of the Catholic Church 964-975; Pope St. John Paul II, Redemptoris Mater
