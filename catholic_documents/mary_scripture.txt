SOURCE: Mary in Sacred Scripture

THE ANNUNCIATION (Luke 1:26-38)
The angel Gabriel appears to Mary, a virgin betrothed to Joseph. Mary is troubled but says, "Behold, I am the handmaid of the Lord. Let it be done to me according to your word." Her fiat (yes) opens the way for the Incarnation.

THE VISITATION (Luke 1:39-56)
Mary visits her cousin Elizabeth. Elizabeth cries out, "Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb!" Mary responds with the Magnificat, praising God's mercy.

THE NATIVITY (Luke 2:1-20)
Mary gives birth to Jesus in Bethlehem. She "kept all these things, pondering them in her heart." Mary is the first to contemplate the mystery of Christ.

THE PRESENTATION (Luke 2:22-38)
Mary and Joseph present Jesus in the Temple. Simeon prophesies that a sword will pierce Mary's soul, foretelling her share in Christ's suffering.

THE FINDING IN THE TEMPLE (Luke 2:41-52)
Mary and Joseph find Jesus teaching in the Temple. Mary says, "Your father and I have been searching for you in great anxiety."

THE WEDDING AT CANA (John 2:1-11)
Mary notices the wine has run out. She tells Jesus, "They have no wine," then instructs the servants, "Do whatever he tells you." Her intercession begins Jesus' public ministry.

AT THE FOOT OF THE CROSS (John 19:25-27)
Jesus entrusts Mary to John and John to Mary: "Woman, behold your son." From the cross, Jesus gives Mary to all of us as our mother.

THE UPPER ROOM (Acts 1:14)
After the Ascension, Mary is present with the apostles in prayer, awaiting the coming of the Holy Spirit.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
From the Annunciation to the Cross to the Upper Room, Mary was faithful to God's plan. She shows us what it means to say "yes" to God every day.

Source: Gospel of Luke; Gospel of John; Acts of the Apostles
