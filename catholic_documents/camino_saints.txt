SOURCE: Saints of the Camino

ST. JAMES THE GREATER
- Patron of pilgrims and Spain
- First apostle to be martyred
- His relics rest in Santiago
- Feast day: July 25

ST. DOMINIC DE LA CALZADA (1019-1109)
- Built bridges, roads, and hospitals for pilgrims
- Patron of civil engineers and pilgrims
- His tomb is in Santo Domingo de la Calzada
- Legend of the cockerel and hen

ST. JOHN OF ORTEGA (c. 1080-1163)
- Monk who built roads and bridges on the Camino
- Provided hospitality to pilgrims
- Patron of the Camino
- His monastery near Burgos

ST. ROCH (c. 1295-1327)
- Pilgrim from Montpellier
- Cared for plague victims
- Depicted with a pilgrim's staff and a wound on his leg
- Patron of pilgrims and the sick

BLESSED ALVARO OF CÓRDOBA (c. 1350-1430)
- Dominican friar who built hospitals for pilgrims
- Known for his devotion to the Eucharist
- Patron of the Camino in Andalusia

ST. FRANCIS OF ASSISI
- Made pilgrimage to Santiago (1214)
- His experience influenced his Rule
- His pilgrimage is commemorated along the Camino

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
These saints made the pilgrimage and built the way for others. They show us that hospitality to pilgrims is hospitality to Christ himself.

Source: Lives of the Saints; Camino Tradition
