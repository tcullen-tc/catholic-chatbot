THE FOUR MARIAN DOGMAS

1. MOTHER OF GOD (Theotokos)
Defined at the Council of Ephesus (431). Mary is truly the Mother of God because she conceived and gave birth to Jesus Christ, who is God the Son. This affirms both the divinity of Christ and Mary's unique role.

2. PERPETUAL VIRGINITY
Mary remained a virgin before, during, and after the birth of Jesus. This teaching is rooted in Scripture and the constant tradition of the Church. She conceived by the Holy Spirit and her virginity remained intact.

3. IMMACULATE CONCEPTION
Defined by Pope Pius IX in 1854 (Ineffabilis Deus). Mary was conceived without original sin. From the first moment of her existence, she was preserved from the stain of sin by the grace of God, in anticipation of her role as Mother of God.

4. ASSUMPTION
Defined by Pope Pius XII in 1950 (Munificentissimus Deus). At the end of her earthly life, Mary was taken body and soul into heavenly glory. She was not subject to bodily decay, sharing in her Son's victory over death.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said to John from the cross, "Behold, your mother." In giving Mary to us, Jesus gave us a mother who is pure, holy, and always ready to lead us to him.
