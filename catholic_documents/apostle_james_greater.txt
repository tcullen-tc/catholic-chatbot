SOURCE: St. James the Greater - Son of Zebedee

KEY FACTS
- Brother of St. John the Evangelist
- Son of Zebedee and Salome
- Called "the Greater" to distinguish him from James the Lesser
- First apostle to be martyred (Acts 12:2)
- Patron saint of Spain, pilgrims, and laborers
- Martyrdom: Beheaded by King Herod Agrippa in Jerusalem (c. 44 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- Called with John: James and John leave their father Zebedee to follow Jesus (Matthew 4:21-22)
- Sons of Thunder: Jesus nicknames James and John "Boanerges," meaning "Sons of Thunder" (Mark 3:17)
- Request for Glory: James and John ask to sit at Jesus' right and left in glory (Mark 10:35-40)
- Transfiguration: James is one of three apostles (with Peter and John) who witness the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:1-8)
- Agony in the Garden: James is present at Gethsemane (Matthew 26:36-46)

TEACHINGS
James wanted greatness but learned that greatness in the Kingdom means drinking the cup of suffering. He was the first apostle to die for his faith.

THE WAY OF ST. JAMES (Camino de Santiago)
James' relics are venerated at Santiago de Compostela in Spain. The pilgrimage route, the Camino de Santiago, has been traveled by millions seeking conversion and spiritual renewal.

FAMOUS QUOTES FROM JESUS TO JAMES
"You do not know what you are asking. Are you able to drink the cup that I drink?" (Mark 10:38)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
James learned that glory in the Kingdom comes through suffering. He drank the cup of martyrdom and now shares in Christ's glory.
