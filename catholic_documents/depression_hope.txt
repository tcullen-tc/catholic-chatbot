WHAT JESUS SAYS TO THE SUFFERING
"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest." (Matthew 11:28)

ST. JOHN PAUL II ON SUFFERING
Suffering is not meaningless. When united with Christ's cross, suffering becomes redemptive. God does not abandon us in our pain; He enters into it.

ST. THERESE OF LISIEUX
"Trust in God. He will not let you fall. He is closer to you than you realize."

HOPE IN DARKNESS
Even in the darkest night, God is present. Depression does not mean God has left you. It is a trial, not a punishment.

YOU ARE NOT ALONE
The Church is a community of support. Do not isolate yourself. Reach out to a priest, a counselor, or a trusted friend.

Sources: Catechism of the Catholic Church 1500-1510; Pope St. John Paul II, Salvifici Doloris
