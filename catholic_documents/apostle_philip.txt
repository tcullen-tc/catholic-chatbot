SOURCE: St. Philip - The Practical Apostle

KEY FACTS
- From Bethsaida, the same town as Peter and Andrew
- Called by Jesus directly (John 1:43-44)
- Known for his practical, questioning nature
- Martyrdom: Crucified upside down in Hierapolis (c. 80 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- The Call: Jesus says, "Follow me," and Philip follows (John 1:43)
- Brings Nathanael: Philip tells Nathanael, "We have found the one Moses wrote about" (John 1:45)
- The Loaves: Jesus asks Philip, "Where shall we buy bread for these people to eat?" Philip calculates the cost (John 6:5-7)
- The Greeks Seek Jesus: Greeks ask Philip, "Sir, we would like to see Jesus" (John 12:21-22)
- Show Us the Father: Philip says, "Lord, show us the Father and that will be enough for us." Jesus responds, "Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father." (John 14:8-9)

TEACHINGS
Philip was practical and sometimes missed the bigger picture. Jesus used his questions to teach deeper truths about faith and his identity.

FAMOUS QUOTES FROM PHILIP
"Come and see." (John 1:46)
"Lord, show us the Father and that will be enough for us." (John 14:8)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Philip shows us that even when we don't fully understand, we can still invite others to "come and see." Jesus meets us in our questions.
