WHAT IS ACTIVE PARTICIPATION?
- Vatican II called for "full, conscious, and active participation" (Sacrosanctum Concilium 14)
- Not just external activity but interior disposition
- Engaging mind, heart, and voice
- It is a right and duty by baptism

INTERIOR PARTICIPATION
- Pay attention to the readings
- Pray with the prayers of the Mass
- Unite your intentions with the priest's
- Enter into silence
- Make the prayers your own

EXTERIOR PARTICIPATION
- Sing the hymns and responses
- Stand, sit, kneel at the proper times
- Receive Communion reverently
- Join in the Sign of Peace
- Give your offering

THE SILENCE OF THE MASS
- Silence after the readings for reflection
- Silence after the homily to ponder
- Silence after Communion for thanksgiving
- Silence before Mass for preparation

OFFERING THE MASS
- Offer the Mass for a particular intention
- Unite your joys and sorrows
- Bring your daily life to the altar
- The Mass is the perfect prayer

PRAYING THE MASS
- Follow in a missal or worship aid
- Pray the prayers silently
- Use the responses meaningfully
- Let the Mass shape your day

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Where two or three are gathered in my name, there am I in the midst of them." Active participation means being truly present with Christ and the assembly.

Source: Sacrosanctum Concilium; General Instruction of the Roman Missal
