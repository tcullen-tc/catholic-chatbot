MAIN ROUTES

THE FRENCH WAY (Camino Francés)
- Most popular route (780 km / 485 miles)
- Starts: St. Jean Pied de Port, France
- Crosses the Pyrenees, through Pamplona, Logroño, Burgos, León
- Takes 30-35 days walking
- Pilgrimage since the 11th century

THE NORTHERN WAY (Camino del Norte)
- Follows the northern coast of Spain (825 km)
- Starts: Irún, near the French border
- Passes through San Sebastián, Bilbao, Santander, Oviedo
- More rugged, fewer pilgrims

THE PORTUGUESE WAY (Camino Portugués)
- Starts: Lisbon (610 km) or Porto (240 km)
- Passes through Barcelos, Ponte de Lima, Tui, Pontevedra
- Second most popular route

THE ENGLISH WAY (Camino Inglés)
- Starts: Ferrol or A Coruña in Galicia
- For pilgrims arriving by sea from England and Ireland
- Shorter route (110-120 km)

THE PRIMITIVE WAY (Camino Primitivo)
- Oldest route, used by King Alfonso II (320 km)
- Starts: Oviedo
- More mountainous and challenging

SPIRITUAL VARIANT
A branch of the Portuguese Way that follows the Ulla River by boat to Padrón, where the boat carrying St. James' body is said to have arrived.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
There are many paths, but all lead to the same destination. As Jesus said, "I am the way." Every route is an opportunity to encounter Christ.
