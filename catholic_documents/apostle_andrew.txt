SOURCE: St. Andrew - The First-Called Apostle

KEY FACTS
- Brother of St. Peter
- Originally a disciple of John the Baptist
- First apostle called by Jesus (John 1:35-40)
- Patron saint of Scotland, Greece, Russia, and fishermen
- Martyrdom: Crucified on an X-shaped cross in Patras, Greece (c. 60 AD)

SCRIPTURE HIGHLIGHTS
- First Called: Andrew hears John the Baptist say, "Behold, the Lamb of God," and follows Jesus (John 1:35-40)
- Brings Peter to Jesus: Andrew immediately finds his brother Simon and brings him to Christ (John 1:41-42)
- The Boy with Loaves and Fishes: Andrew brings the boy with five loaves and two fish to Jesus (John 6:8-9)
- Greeks Seek Jesus: Andrew and Philip bring Greeks who want to see Jesus (John 12:20-22)

TEACHINGS
Andrew shows us the importance of bringing others to Christ. He is always introducing people to Jesus—first his brother Peter, then the boy with the loaves, then the Greeks.

PATRONAGE
Andrew is the patron saint of fishermen (because he was a fisherman), of Russia (where he is said to have preached), and of Scotland (where his relics were brought).

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Andrew first found his own brother Simon and said to him, 'We have found the Messiah.'" (John 1:41)
"There is a boy here who has five barley loaves and two fish." (John 6:9)

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Andrew did not do great miracles like Peter, but he brought people to Jesus. Every person who brings another to Christ is doing the work of an apostle.
