SOURCE: Our Lady of Guadalupe (1531)

THE APPARITION
Our Lady appeared to St. Juan Diego, a poor indigenous man, on Tepeyac Hill near Mexico City from December 9-12, 1531. She identified herself as the "Mother of the True God."

THE MIRACLE OF THE ROSES
When Bishop Zumárraga demanded a sign, Mary arranged roses (which were out of season) in Juan Diego's tilma (cloak). When Juan Diego opened his tilma before the bishop, the roses fell and the image of Our Lady was miraculously imprinted on the cloth.

THE IMAGE
The tilma of Juan Diego, with the image of Our Lady, remains miraculously preserved after nearly 500 years. Scientific studies have found:
- No trace of paint or pigment
- The image is inexplicable by natural means
- The stars on her mantle reflect the constellations of December 12, 1531
- Her eyes reflect images of those present at the miracle

MESSAGE
Mary came as a mother to all peoples, especially the poor and indigenous. She asked for a church to be built where all could find her love and protection. Millions converted to the faith following the apparition.

PATRONESS
Our Lady of Guadalupe is the Patroness of the Americas and the Star of Evangelization.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Behold, your mother." At Guadalupe, Mary showed herself as the mother of all peoples, bringing hope and faith to a new world.
