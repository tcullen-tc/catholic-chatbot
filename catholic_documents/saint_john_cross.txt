SOURCE: St. John of the Cross (1542-1591)
Doctor of the Church

KEY TEACHINGS

DARK NIGHT OF THE SOUL
St. John wrote about the "dark night" – periods of spiritual dryness and purification that lead to deeper union with God. He taught that these trials are gifts of grace, not punishments.

THE ASCENT OF MOUNT CARMEL
John described the spiritual journey as climbing Mount Carmel. He taught that attachment to anything less than God hinders our progress. Detachment leads to freedom.

LIVING FLAME OF LOVE
His poetry and commentary on the "Living Flame of Love" describe the soul's ecstatic union with God. He showed that suffering, when united with Christ, becomes a path to joy.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"In the evening of life, we will be judged on love alone."
"Where there is no love, put love, and you will find love."
"The soul that walks in love neither tires others nor grows tired."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "If any man would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me." St. John teaches us that the cross leads to resurrection and darkness leads to light.

Sources: Dark Night of the Soul; Ascent of Mount Carmel; Living Flame of Love
