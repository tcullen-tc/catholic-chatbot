SOURCE: The Catholic Mass - Source and Summit of Faith

WHAT IS THE MASS?
The Mass is the celebration of the Eucharist, the memorial of Christ's death and resurrection. It is the central act of Catholic worship, where the faithful gather to hear God's Word and receive the Body and Blood of Christ.

SOURCE AND SUMMIT
The Catechism calls the Eucharist the "source and summit" of Christian life (CCC 1324). From the Mass flows all grace; to the Mass all Christian life is ordered.

NAMES FOR THE MASS
- Eucharist: Greek for "thanksgiving"
- Lord's Supper: Remembrance of the Last Supper
- Breaking of Bread: Early Christian name
- Holy Sacrifice: The sacrifice of Christ made present
- Holy Mass: From the Latin "Ite, missa est" (Go, you are sent)

TWO GREAT PARTS
- Liturgy of the Word: God speaks to us through Scripture
- Liturgy of the Eucharist: We respond with the offering of Christ

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "Do this in memory of me." At the Last Supper, he gave us the Mass as the perpetual memorial of his sacrifice. In every Mass, we are present at Calvary.

Source: Catechism of the Catholic Church 1322-1419
