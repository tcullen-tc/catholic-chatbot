SOURCE: St. Thomas More (1478-1535)
Martyr, Lord Chancellor of England

KEY TEACHINGS

INTEGRITY IN PUBLIC LIFE
Thomas More served as Lord Chancellor of England but resigned rather than compromise his faith. He chose death rather than betray the Church.

CONSCIENCE ABOVE POWER
More refused to sign the Act of Supremacy, which made the king head of the Church in England. He said: "I am the king's good servant, but God's first."

WIT IN SUFFERING
Even in prison and facing death, More kept his sense of humor. He showed that faith gives joy even in the darkest moments.

FAMOUS QUOTES
"I die the king's good servant, but God's first."
"One of the greatest problems of our time is that we do not know what we are not allowed to question."
"Pray for me as I will for you, that we may merrily meet in heaven."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus said, "What does it profit a man to gain the whole world and forfeit his soul?" St. Thomas More teaches us that no earthly power is worth losing our soul. We must follow our conscience, even when it costs everything.
