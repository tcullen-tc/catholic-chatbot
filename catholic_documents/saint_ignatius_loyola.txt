SOURCE: St. Ignatius of Loyola (1491-1556)
Founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits)

KEY TEACHINGS

THE SPIRITUAL EXERCISES
St. Ignatius developed the Spiritual Exercises, a retreat structure for discernment and spiritual growth. The goal is to help a person find God's will and grow closer to Christ.

PRINCIPLE AND FOUNDATION
"Man is created to praise, reverence, and serve God our Lord, and by this means to save his soul. The other things on the face of the earth are created for man to help him in attaining the end for which he is created."

DISCERNMENT OF SPIRITS
Ignatius taught how to distinguish between the movements of the good spirit (which brings peace, hope, and love) and the evil spirit (which brings disturbance, fear, and discouragement).

FAMOUS QUOTES
"Pray as if everything depends on God. Work as if everything depends on you."
"Go forth and set the world on fire."
"Love is shown more by deeds than by words."

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus calls us to leave everything and follow him. He said, "If any man would come after me, let him deny himself and take up his cross and follow me." St. Ignatius teaches us to seek God's will in all things.

Sources: Spiritual Exercises of St. Ignatius; Ignatian Spirituality
