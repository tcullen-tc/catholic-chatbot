ST. JAMES THE GREATER
- Son of Zebedee, brother of St. John
- First apostle to be martyred (Acts 12:2)
- Beheaded by King Herod Agrippa in Jerusalem (c. 44 AD)

THE DISCOVERY (813 AD)
A hermit named Pelayo saw mysterious lights over a forest. Bishop Theodomirus investigated and discovered an ancient tomb with three bodies. They were identified as St. James and two of his disciples.

RECOGNITION
King Alfonso II declared St. James the patron saint of Spain. He built the first church over the tomb. Pilgrims began arriving almost immediately.

MEDIEVAL PILGRIMAGE
- One of the three great pilgrimages of Christendom
- Pilgrims traveled from all over Europe
- Provided spiritual benefits, including indulgences
- Created a network of roads, hospitals, churches, and hostels

THE SCALLOP SHELL
- Traditional symbol of St. James
- Pilgrims wear it as a badge
- Shells were found on the beaches of Galicia
- The grooves represent the many routes converging at Santiago

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
The discovery of St. James' tomb was a sign that his witness continued. Just as James drank the cup of martyrdom, pilgrims drink the cup of pilgrimage as a spiritual journey.
