WHAT IS A HOLY YEAR?
A Holy Year (Xacobeo or Jubilee Year) occurs when the feast of St. James (July 25) falls on a Sunday. Pilgrims who visit the Cathedral during a Holy Year can receive a plenary indulgence.

DATES OF HOLY YEARS
- 2021 was the most recent Holy Year
- Next Holy Years: 2027, 2032, 2038, 2049, 2055
- The tradition began in the 12th century

THE HOLY DOOR (Puerta Santa)
- Opened only during Holy Years
- Located on the east side of the Cathedral
- Pilgrims enter through the Holy Door to receive the indulgence
- The door is walled shut after the Holy Year

CONDITIONS FOR THE INDULGENCE
- Visit the Cathedral of Santiago
- Enter through the Holy Door (during Holy Year)
- Pray for the Pope's intentions
- Receive the sacrament of Confession
- Receive Holy Communion
- Walk the Camino or make the pilgrimage with true repentance

THE JUBILEE BLESSING
The Jubilee is a time of grace, forgiveness, and renewal. It recalls the biblical tradition of jubilee years when debts were forgiven and captives freed.

WHAT JESUS WOULD SAY:
Jesus proclaimed a year of favor. The Holy Year is an invitation to return to the Lord with all your heart, to receive mercy, and to begin anew.
